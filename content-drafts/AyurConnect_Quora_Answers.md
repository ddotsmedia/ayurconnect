## 1. What are the best Ayurvedic treatments for PCOS?

PCOS is well-matched to Ayurveda's individualised approach because the condition presents so differently from person to person — some women have predominantly metabolic symptoms (insulin resistance, weight), others have androgen-dominant presentations (hair loss, acne), and others have irregular cycles as the primary complaint.

Classically, Ayurveda frames PCOS-like conditions under *Artava Kshaya* (menstrual insufficiency) and *Granthi* (cystic formations), with *Kapha* and *Vata* as the primary dosha imbalances involved.

**Most supported interventions:**

- **Lodhra (*Symplocos racemosa*)** — A 2007 clinical study found 3 g/day significantly reduced LH levels and improved FSH/LH ratios over 12 weeks. This is one of the better-evidenced single herbs in PCOS research.
- **Kanchanara Guggulu** — The classical compound for ovarian cyst management. Widely used in clinical Ayurveda.
- **Shatavari** — Female reproductive tonic; well-tolerated and supports ovulatory function.
- **Guduchi** — Has insulin-sensitising properties comparable to metformin in early research.
- **Virechana Panchakarma** — Systemic detoxification that addresses the metabolic environment. Requires physician supervision.
- **Uttara Basti** — Localised uterine treatment; significant clinical evidence from Ayurveda college research.

**Important:** Herbs alone are not sufficient — dietary changes (reducing *Kapha*-aggravating foods), regular vigorous exercise, and consistent sleep schedules are equal pillars of treatment in Ayurveda.

The evidence base is growing but not yet equivalent to mainstream medical standards for most individual herbs. Always work with a qualified BAMS physician rather than self-prescribing, especially if you are trying to conceive or managing concurrent metabolic conditions.

## 2. Is BAMS a good career option in 2026?

Honestly — yes, but with realistic expectations about the trajectory.

BAMS in 2026 is a better career investment than it was a decade ago, for several reasons:

**What's improved:**
- AYUSH Ministry's budget has grown substantially, and government AMO (Ayurveda Medical Officer) posts are expanding across states. Kerala PSC BAMS salary is ₹56,100–₹95,600/month with pension — genuinely competitive with comparable government roles.
- International demand is real. DHA-licensed Ayurveda physicians in Dubai earn AED 8,000–18,000/month (tax-free). UK and Germany are also developing markets.
- Telemedicine has removed geographic constraints — BAMS graduates can build remote practices serving diaspora communities.
- The wellness industry is growing rapidly and employs BAMS physicians at resort medical director level (₹40,000–₹90,000/month).

**What's still challenging:**
- Entry-level private sector salaries (₹18,000–₹30,000 for fresh BAMS) are low given the 5.5-year programme length.
- Government job competition is intense — 40–60 candidates per seat for Kerala PSC AMO.
- Telemedicine and international practice require additional qualifications (Prometric exam, DHA license) that take 6–12 months to obtain.

**The honest answer:** BAMS rewards those who either pursue PG (MD/MS significantly improves all career outcomes) or develop a specific specialisation like Panchakarma, or who are willing to invest in the 12–18-month process for government exam or international licensure.

For those who want clinical medicine but not the extremely competitive MBBS track, BAMS offers a legitimate and increasingly respected alternative.

## 3. How can I get DHA license as an Ayurveda doctor?

The DHA (Dubai Health Authority) license is the required credential for Ayurveda physicians practicing in Dubai emirate. Here is the process in order:

**Step 1 — Dataflow Primary Source Verification**
Your BAMS certificate, mark sheets, internship completion certificate, and CCIM/state medical council registration are verified through Dataflow Group (commissioned by DHA). Submit through the Dataflow portal. Timeline: 6–10 weeks. Cost: approximately AED 1,400–1,700.

**Step 2 — Prometric Exam**
DHA uses a computer-based Prometric examination for Ayurveda physician licensure. The exam covers Ayurvedic clinical subjects, anatomy, physiology, pathology, and clinical medicine. Pass mark is typically 60%. The exam can be taken in India at authorised Prometric centres (available in most major cities). Cost: AED 1,000–1,200.

**Step 3 — Good Standing Letter**
Obtain a certificate of good standing from your state medical council (KSMC if you are registered in Kerala). This confirms no disciplinary proceedings.

**Step 4 — DHA Portal Application**
Once Dataflow is complete and exam is passed, submit the application through the Salama portal (DHA's professional licensing system). You will need a UAE employer (sponsor) at this stage — the license is employer-linked initially.

**Step 5 — License Issuance**
Upon employment confirmation and visa, DHA issues the full license.

**Total cost:** Approximately AED 3,000–3,500
**Total timeline:** 4–8 months from starting Dataflow to license in hand

Tip: Start the Dataflow process while still in India — it runs concurrently with job searching and cuts 2–3 months off your timeline.

## 4. What is Panchakarma treatment and how much does it cost in Kerala?

Panchakarma — literally "five actions" in Sanskrit — is Ayurveda's systematic detoxification and rejuvenation therapy. The five classical procedures are *Vamana* (therapeutic emesis), *Virechana* (purgation), *Basti* (medicated enema), *Nasya* (nasal administration), and *Raktamokshana* (bloodletting). In practice, most contemporary programmes focus on *Virechana*, *Basti*, and *Nasya* alongside preparatory therapies like full-body oil massage (*Abhyanga*) and steam (*Sweda*).

It is a medical procedure requiring physician supervision, not a spa experience — though some premium retreats blur this line.

**Cost in Kerala 2026:**

- **Government Ayurveda hospitals:** ₹25,000–₹50,000 for a 7-day residential programme. Clinically rigorous, staffed by qualified BAMS/MD physicians. No-frills environment.
- **Established private hospitals** (Kottakkal AVS, Sitaram, Nagarjuna, Vaidyaratnam): ₹80,000–₹1,80,000 for 14 days. Good balance of clinical quality and amenity.
- **Premium wellness retreats** (health tourism-oriented): ₹1,80,000–₹3,00,000+ for 14–21 days. International standards of accommodation, curated meals, often a resort environment.

**Key questions to ask before booking:**
1. Is internal oleation (*Snehapana*) included? Without it, it's not classical Panchakarma.
2. Who supervises daily — a physician or a therapist?
3. What post-treatment (*Paschatkarma*) guidance is included?
4. Where are the medicines sourced from — GMP-certified pharmacy?

Government hospitals are underrated. For many therapeutic purposes, they offer equivalent clinical quality to premium retreats at a fraction of the cost.

## 5. How to find a good Ayurveda doctor online?

Finding a qualified Ayurveda doctor online takes more care than it might seem, because the label "Ayurveda doctor" or "Ayurveda practitioner" is used by people with vastly different qualifications — from 5.5-year BAMS graduates registered with the Central Council of Indian Medicine to wellness coaches with a weekend certification.

Here is what to check:

**1. BAMS degree verification**
A qualified Ayurveda physician in India holds a Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) degree from a CCIM-recognised institution. Ask for the institution and graduation year; it takes 30 seconds to cross-reference.

**2. CCIM or State Board registration**
In India, practicing without registration is illegal. The doctor should display their CCIM or state medical council registration number (in Kerala, KSMC). This number should appear on their prescription.

**3. Teleconsultation legitimacy**
India's 2020 Telemedicine Practice Guidelines authorise BAMS physicians to consult remotely. A legitimate online consultation should include a proper video call (not just a chat), a prescription with the doctor's registration number, and a clinical history-taking process.

**4. Red flags to avoid**
- "Cure guaranteed" claims
- Secret ingredient formulas with undisclosed compositions
- Very short consultations (under 15 minutes) that jump directly to selling products
- No registration number on prescriptions

**5. Specialisation match**
Ayurveda has recognised specialisations. If you need Panchakarma, a physician with MD in Panchakarma is better qualified than a general BAMS for complex cases. Match the specialty to your condition.
